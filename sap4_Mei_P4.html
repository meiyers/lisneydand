The Gift Shop

Mei Lin Meyers

Here's back to the start

They run their hands laboriously over pure jade stone. Forming pearlescent light into a tangible body, rendering itself useful.
Useless. Hurting eyes. I fumble. Shards of jade slipping out of my fingers.

The fragments call out to their mother, to the fictitious voices that coalesce. They descend, lonesome detritus longing to find resolution and dissolve in an embrace.
However, in returning to the arms of their genesis, they seize. They look away. Missing the comfort in falling. The obscurity and warmth of the sky.
Without a reference is falling not just floating? Drifting through smog. Cramped hands. Be better.
